History of Minecraft

Minecraft was initially created bt Markus "Notch" Persson, a Swedish game programmers. Persson has worked for King for over four years until 2009, when he began full-time development on Minecraft. His idea brewed from a game he was developing titled "RubyDung". With the first-person perspective, blocky visuals, and building elements from Infiniminer, and the RPG elements/base building from RubyDung, Minecraft was born.

Markus stayed with the growing team at Mojang (he was the founding member) until September 2014, when Microsoft announced a $2.5 billion acquisition deal to purchase the company. From there, he decided to step away from game development. The lead designer of Minecraft since 2011 has been Jens "Jeb" Bergensten, who has been co-developing Minecraft with Persson since 2010, took the lead designer role in 2011, and assumed full creative control in 2014 upon Persson's departure.

Java Edition is the original version of Minecraft and began development in 2009 with various Alphas and Betas leading through 2010 to the beginning of 2011. The full version, titled "1.0 - Adventure Update" released on November 18th 2011. Making Minecraft 14 years old from its initial conception, and 12 years old from its full public release.

Major Updates

Some major updates Minecraft from 20212 (first major updates) has had are 1.4 Pretty Scary update and 1.4 Redstone update. In 2013, an update came out that changed the world, which focused almost entirely on revamping the world generation systems, and introduced 11 new biomes in the process. Beginning of 2016 came the combat update, which introduced the off-hand slot so players could dual wield, implemented a cooldown mechanic for attacks, added the shield item and the Elytra, which enabled flying/gliding within Survival mode. This updated also revamped The End, so that the outer islands were populated by end city structures and the new Shulker mob to defeat. In 2017 they released World of Color update, adding a large number of colored blocks to the game. In 2018 came the Aquatic update, which introduced colorful coral reefs and mobs like turtle, dolphin, cod, salmon, and pufferfish. 2019 brought the Village and Pillage update to players, altering the way villages spawned in-game, and the purposes that they could serve. The Nether update was released in 2020, introducing new biomes as well as the addition of the Netherite items gave players all the more reason to delve deep into the underworld. Caves and Cliffs was a two-part mega update for Minecraft in 2021. The first update (1.17), introduced the axolotl, glow squid, and goat mobs, as well as some unique plant life such as the Glow Lichen and the Dripleaf. Underground, you can now find copper ore and amethyst geodes to mine. Part 2 of Caves and Cliffs update altered the way world generation works in-game, adding not only overworld biomes like the Grove and the Meadow, but overhual of mountains and cave systems. There are also unique areas underground like the Lush or Dripstone cave biomes. As for 2023, the Trails and Tales update introduces bamboo, cherry wood set, decorated pots, and a total of 11 sign variants (including the ability to hang signs). There are also smithing templates that allows you to add trims to your armor anf unique mobs like the camel and sniffer that present brand new gameplay opportunities.

Survival vs. Creative

Creative mode offers unlimited resources for building and exploration. Hunger and health does not go down in creative mode. Creative is for anyone who wants a stress-free experience or to spend some time figuring out the basics. Though in creative you cannot earn achievements.

Survival mode provides a better experience for players who value challenge, immersion, progression, and rewarding gameplay. It challenges players to gather resources, fend off hostile mobs, and manage their health and hunger while exploring the sandbox. Players gradually build their way up, unlocking new weapons, tools, materials, and abilities as they advance. You can earn achievements in survival mode.

Fun Facts

  • Persson's avatar in game is the only one who drops an apple when he dies.
  • Around 126 million people play Minecraft every month.
  • The Enderman language is the English language backwards, and sometimes just at a lower pitch.
  • One day in Minecraft is 20 minutes in real-time
  • There are six types of mob: the utility mob, boss mob, hostile mob, neutral mob, tamable mob, and the passive mob
  • The higher a wolf's tail in the game, the healthier it is
