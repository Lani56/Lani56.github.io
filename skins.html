Skins

What Are Skins?

Skins are custom images that change the appearance of your in-game character. They allow players to express their creativity and personality by altering the character's look, from default skins like Steve and ALex to unique designs created by community. Skins are typically in PNG format and can be downloaded or uploaded to change your character's appearance in the game. Players can choose from a variety of skins, including those inspired by popular culture or characters, and even create their own skins to showcase their style. You can also purchase skins/skin packs from the Minecraft store.

How To Change Your Skins

Java Edition (For custom skins)

  • Go to the change your skin page in your minecraft profile
  • Choose either classic or slim model
  • Scroll down to Upload Custom Skin and click Select File to choose your skin file
  • Click Upload and then open the Minecraft Launcher

Bedrock Edition

  • Click on the Dressing Room option under your character model
  • Go to Edit Character/Create Character. A pop-up menu will appear and you can choose between Character Skins or Classic Skins.
  • Select Character Skins if you want to customize every aspect of your character's avatar
  • Select Classic Skins if you want to buy outfits made by other users or upload your own
  • To upload your own skin, go to Classic Skins → Owned Skins → Choose New Skin
  • Upload the item and save changes to equip it in the game

Pocket Edition

  • Download the skin you want to wear from websites like The Skindex
  • Launch Minecraft and go to the Dressing Room
  • Tap on the three bars in the top left corner of the screen
  • Select Classic skins → Owned → Gray skin → choose new skin
  • Upload the skin in the correct format and dimensions to use it in the game

While you cannot import third-part Minecraft skins on console, you can still purchase them through the in-game dressing room feature.
